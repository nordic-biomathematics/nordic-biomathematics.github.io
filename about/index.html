About the Meeting

Mathematical Biology in the Nordic Region

Mathematical biology is a rapidly growing field that uses mathematical models, theory, and computational methods to understand biological systems across scales — from molecules and cells to populations and ecosystems. It encompasses a wide range of approaches including differential equations, stochastic processes, statistical inference, evolutionary theory, and machine learning, applied to questions in physiology, ecology, evolution, epidemiology, and beyond.

The Nordic countries host a strong and diverse community in mathematical biology and biomathematics. However, interactions between researchers often remain local or restricted to specific subfields. There is no dedicated recurring regional forum where the broader community — spanning Denmark, Finland, Iceland, Norway, and Sweden — can meet, exchange ideas, and build lasting collaborations across institutions, research areas, and career stages.

A Nordic Forum for Biomathematics

The 3rd Nordic Biomathematics Meeting aims to fill this gap. It builds on the tradition of previous Swedish and Nordic biomathematics meetings, and has the explicit goal of restarting a recurring regional forum that brings the community together on a regular basis. By gathering researchers from across the Nordic countries at different career stages, the meeting aims to foster exchange, catalyse new collaborations, and strengthen the visibility and coherence of the field in the region.

The meeting is open to researchers at all career stages — from PhD students to senior professors — working in mathematical biology, biomathematics, or adjacent areas of applied mathematics, statistics, and theoretical biology.
