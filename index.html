The 3rd Nordic Biomathematics Meeting

The meeting brings together researchers in mathematical biology and biomathematics across the Nordic countries, with the aim of fostering scientific exchange and collaboration across institutions, research areas, and career stages.

About

Mathematical biology is a rapidly growing field that uses mathematical models, theory, and computational methods to understand biological systems across scales. The Nordic countries host a strong and diverse community in this area, but a dedicated regional forum has been lacking.

The 3rd Nordic Biomathematics Meeting aims to restart a recurring Nordic forum for researchers in mathematical biology and biomathematics — bringing together scientists from Denmark, Finland, Iceland, Norway, and Sweden at all career stages.

Programme Preliminary

The meeting will take place over two days and include:

  • 5 plenary talks
  • 14 contributed talks
  • Poster session with 1-minute lightning talks
  • Symposium dinner on the evening of 3 December

A detailed timetable will be published closer to the meeting.
